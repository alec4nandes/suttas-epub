Introduction

Our fast-paced world offers few refuges. Let the Buddha's words be your sanctuary.

The Ānāpānassati, Bhaddekaratta, and Mahāsatipaṭṭhāna Suttas contain lists and concepts that every devoted practitioner should know. Read them often — a consistent practice settles our minds and opens our hearts. In his book The Heart of the Buddha's Teaching, Thich Nhat Hanh recommends reading these 3 special suttas every week. That's why we put them all in one place, in a digital format that's easily adjustable for any reader.

Another highly recommended sutta not included in this collection is the Vitakkasaṇṭhāna Sutta on how to stop thinking.

Bhikkhu Sujato translated these texts and we have annotated them further to show how they relate. Explore this book page by page, or take a non-linear approach by jumping to the Notes section. The links there will lead you to deeper explanations in the appropriate texts.

With that said, please sit back, tune into your breath, unclench your jaw, and read deeply into the wisdom of the Dharma.
